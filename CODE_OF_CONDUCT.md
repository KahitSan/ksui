# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make taking part in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, no matter their age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, social and economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that build an open, welcoming, diverse,
inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps make our community a good place:

* Showing kindness and patience toward other people
* Respecting different opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving helpful feedback, and accepting it with grace
* Taking responsibility, apologizing to those we affect by our mistakes, and
  learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best for the whole community, not just for ourselves

Examples of behavior we do not allow:

* Sexual language or imagery, and unwanted sexual attention of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or demeaning comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Sharing other people's private information, such as a home or email address,
  without their clear permission
* Other conduct that would reasonably be seen as inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for making our standards of acceptable
behavior clear, and they will take fair and appropriate action in response to
any behavior that they find harmful, offensive, or otherwise out of line.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do
not follow this Code of Conduct. They will explain their reasons for a
moderation decision when it is helpful to do so.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces. It also applies when a
person is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of
representing our community include using an official email address, posting from
an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an
online or offline event.

## Enforcement

You can report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior to the
KahitSan team. The best way is to open a private security advisory on the
[KahitSan/ksui](https://github.com/KahitSan/ksui/security/advisories) repository,
or contact the team directly. All reports will be reviewed and looked into
promptly and fairly.

All community leaders must respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports
an incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines when they decide
on the consequences for any action they find breaks this Code of Conduct.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior seen as
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, with some
clarity about why the behavior was not okay. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A single incident, or a series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning, with rules for future behavior. No interaction with
the people involved, including unrequested contact with those who enforce the
Code of Conduct, for a set period of time. Breaking these terms may lead to a
temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious break of community standards, including
continued behavior that is not okay.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any kind of interaction or public
communication with the community for a set period of time. Breaking these terms
may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Showing a pattern of breaking community standards,
including continued bad behavior, harassment of a person, or aggression toward
or putting down groups of people.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any kind of public interaction within the
community.
